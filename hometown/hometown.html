Bontang, East Kalimantan

Bontang is a city on the eastern coast of the island of Borneo in Indonesia, in the province of East Kalimantan. It occupies an area of 497.57 km2 (192.11 sq mi), and the population was 140,787 at the 2010 Census, and the 2017 Civil Registry estimated the population of the city as 170,611. In 2010, Bontang is a municipality's highest GDP (nominal) per capita in Indonesia, with Rp375,407,000 (US$38,306) according to Statistics Indonesia. However, the income and GDP per capita trend always decrease since 2015 due to decline of coal mining and LNG production sector impact to negative economic growth.
